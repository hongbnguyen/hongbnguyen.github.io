Seeing Forces · Published · Cognition 2024

Rotating objects cue spatial attention via frictive surface contact

Nguyen, H. B., and van Buren, B. (2024). Rotating objects cue spatial attention. Cognition, 242, 105655.PDF

We report a new attentional cueing effect showing how attention models friction. A clockwise-rotating wheel on the floor would roll to the right — does the visual system encode this, and does it automatically shift attention rightward in anticipation? We found that it does: clockwise rotation cues rightward attention, counterclockwise cues leftward. And when the wheel touches a ceiling instead, the effect fully reverses.

Demonstrations

Experiment 1 — Rotating objects cue spatial attention

Clockwise rotation produced faster responses to targets on the right, counterclockwise to the left.

Experiment 2 — Reversal by ceiling contact

Floor-touching showed cueing, floor-not-touching abolished it, ceiling-touching reversed it!
