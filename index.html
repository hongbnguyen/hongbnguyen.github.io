About

Hi, I'm Hong B. Nguyen, a vision scientist who studies how surprisingly sophisticated implicit knowledge is quietly woven into how we perceive patterns of light landing on our retinas.

My work shows that the visual system makes unconscious assumptions about invisible physical forces such as gravity and friction, allowing us to anticipate motion before we explicitly reason about it. I’ve also found that we automatically extract social meaning from movement and facial expressions — seeing animacy, agency, and intention with remarkable speed. A third branch of my research investigates how visual attention and information acquisition interact with cognition in complex tasks, including navigation and aesthetic experience.

Overall, my work supports two important conclusions — that fast, automatic visual processing is “smarter” than we typically give it credit for, and that seemingly simple acts of looking and seeing provide a rich foundation for higher-level thought.
